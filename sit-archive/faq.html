Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about the archive. Can't find the answer you're looking for? Reach out to our support team.

Is SIT Archive free?

expand_more

Yes, SIT Archive is a completely free resource provided exclusively for SIT students. Our goal is to facilitate the sharing and accessing of academic materials without any cost barriers.

Who can upload papers?

expand_more

Any verified student with a valid SIT email address can upload papers. We encourage community contributions to keep our repository up-to-date with the latest exam papers and study materials.

Why can only SIT emails log in?

expand_more

To ensure the integrity of our platform and comply with academic guidelines, access is restricted to currently enrolled students. This helps us maintain a secure environment for sharing internal academic resources.

Are uploads verified?

expand_more

Yes. All uploaded documents undergo a moderation process by our student volunteer team. We check for relevance, quality, and accurate labeling before the files become publicly available in the browse section.

Can I report incorrect papers?

expand_more

Absolutely. If you find a paper that is mislabeled, low quality, or copyrighted material that shouldn't be here, please use the "Report" flag located on the document viewer page.

Can I request a missing paper?

expand_more

Yes! We have a "Request" feature in the dashboard. You can post a request for a specific module or year, and other students who might have it will be notified.

Is my data safe?

expand_more

We take privacy seriously. We only collect the minimal information needed to verify your student status. We do not sell your data or share your browsing history with the university administration.

Why is dark mode the default?

expand_more

Most students study late at night. We designed SIT Archive with a "dark-mode-first" philosophy to reduce eye strain and provide a more comfortable reading experience during those long study sessions.
